About SORET

The South Rift Evangelistic Team (SORET) is a dedicated Christian organization committed to spreading the Gospel of Jesus Christ. We believe in the transformative power of God's Word and are passionate about making Christ known in communities.

SORET operates in collaboration with the University of Eldoret Christian Union and SORET Kenya leadership. This partnership ensures that our evangelistic efforts are grounded in strong theological principles and supported by established Christian guidance. We are committed to reaching individuals and communities with the message of hope, salvation, and eternal life found only in Jesus Christ.

Our work involves organizing and conducting evangelistic missions, outreach programs, and discipleship initiatives to nurture new believers. We strive to embody the teachings of the Bible in our actions, reflecting God's love and compassion in every interaction.

Our Mission & Vision

Our Mission

Taking the gospel to the south rift regions and beyond, to make Christ known through evangelism. We are dedicated to actively engaging communities with the life-changing message of the Gospel, fostering spiritual growth, and inviting individuals into a relationship with Jesus Christ.

Our Vision

To ensure the entire south rift region and beyond are reached with the gospel. We envision a future where every individual in our target regions has had the opportunity to hear, understand, and respond to the good news of salvation.

ARTICLE 3: DOCTRINAL BASIS

  1. The belief in the trinity of God-head (God –The Father, Son, Holy-spirit).
  2. The sovereignty of God in creation, revelation and final judgement.
  3. The divine inspiration and the entire trustworthiness of the holy scriptures as originally given by God and its supreme authority in all matters of faith and conduct.
  4. The universal sinfulness and guilt of all men since the fall of man and rendering them to Gods’ wrath and condemnation.
  5. Redemption from guilt penalty dominion and pollution of sin solely through the sacrificial death of the Lord Jesus Christ, the incarnate son of God.
  6. The virgin birth of Jesus Christ, his death on the cross, his resurrection, his ascension to the right hand of the father and in expectation of His personal return in power and glory.
  7. The presence and the power of the Holy-spirit in the work of regeneration and the indwelling in every believer.
  8. The justification of the sinner by the grace of God through faith in Jesus Christ alone.
  9. The holy universal church in the body of Christ unto which all true believers belong.
  10. Existence of the devil whose ultimate and his eternal condemnation.
  11. The baptism on water, of the Holy Spirit and by fire.

ARTICLE 4: MEMBERSHIP

4.1 Normal membership

Christians from South Rift or elsewhere who apart from sharing the same aims of SORET consistently participate in SORET activities.

4.2 Associate membership.

It shall be open to all born again alumni students who will be willing to participate in all SORET activities and adhere to practice the Christian faith as given in doctrinal basis.

4.3 Subscription

There shall be no membership fee charged on the full members, however, associates are required to contribute an annual subscription fee of Ksh 500 for renewal of their membership. All other members will contribute to the SORET treasury as the Lord will lead them.

ARTICLE 5: MEMBERSHIP RIGHTS AND RESPONSIBILITIES.

5.1 Membership rights

  • Eligible to hold office and participate in the elections of the officials of SORET.
  • Entitled to requisition for and vote in any general meeting.
  • Capable of proposing and effecting alterations, modification or other amendments to this constitution which when need be shall be amended in a period not less than 3 to 5 years.

5.2 Membership Responsibilities

  • All members shall be expected to contribute to SORET treasury as the Lords leads them.
  • All members must actively participate in SORET activities.

5.3 Loss of Membership

  • When one has gone against the doctrinal basis of SORET.
  • When one has gone against the expectation of SORET code of conduct.
  • On voluntary resignation by a member via writing.
